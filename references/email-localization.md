# Email Localization

Choose the outreach language from evidence, not just country.

## Language Priority

1. Contact page language.
2. Company website primary language.
3. Country or region business language.
4. English fallback.

Prefer English when local-language confidence is low, when the company already uses English for international trade, or when the product is technical and the lead appears export/import oriented.

## Common Defaults

| Region | Recommended first choice | Fallback |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Germany, Austria | German if evidence is strong | English |
| France | French if evidence is strong | English |
| Spain, Mexico, Latin America | Spanish | English |
| Poland | Polish if evidence is strong | English |
| UAE, Saudi Arabia, Gulf trade hubs | English | Arabic |
| Netherlands, Nordics | English | Local language |
| Taiwan, Hong Kong | Traditional Chinese or English by website language | English |
| Japan, Korea | Local language for domestic SMEs | English |

## Tone

Use a concise B2B trade style:

- Specific reason for contacting them.
- One sentence about seller capability.
- One sentence connecting the product to their market/channel.
- Low-pressure CTA.
- No hype, exaggerated claims, fake urgency, or invented familiarity.

## Personalization Inputs

Use only source-backed facts:

- Product categories on their website.
- Distributor/importer/wholesaler role.
- Brands or adjacent lines they already carry.
- Industries or applications they serve.
- Contact page, catalog, or brochure evidence.

If personalization evidence is weak, set `send_recommendation=review` and explain the gap in `risk_flags`.
